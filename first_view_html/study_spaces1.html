Study Spaces

Find Your Ideal Study Spot

Discover the best places on campus to focus and collaborate on coursework.

Shapiro Undergraduate Library

The Shapiro Library, officially known as the Shapiro Undergraduate Library, is a central resource for University of Michigan students, offering study spaces, group study rooms, computer workstations, and the Academic Innovation Lab. The library also hosts workshops and events to enhance research skills and academic success.

Located at 919 South University Avenue, Shapiro Library is easily accessible from central campus and public transportation routes.

Hatcher Graduate Library

The Hatcher Graduate Library is a significant academic resource, especially for graduate students. Located at 913 South University Avenue, Hatcher offers access to a vast collection of resources and specialized subject librarians. It’s known for its quiet study areas and specialized archives for in-depth research.

The library is located near the Diag and Burton Memorial Tower, with easy access via campus buses and within walking distance of key campus locations.

Duderstadt Center

The Duderstadt Center, or "the Dude," is a hub for creativity, technology, and innovation. Located on North Campus, it houses the Digital Media Commons and provides study spaces, computer workstations, and advanced labs for video, audio, and 3D work. It's ideal for students engaged in creative projects and interdisciplinary work.

The center is easily accessible via university buses or by car, with nearby parking options.

North Quad Spaces

North Quad is a vibrant community that combines residential life with academic spaces. Home to the School of Information and several other departments, it offers modern study spaces and collaborative environments for students. The building is located on the corner of State Street and Huron Street, easily accessible by foot from central campus.

With its distinctive architecture, North Quad fosters interdisciplinary exchange and a dynamic living-learning experience.
